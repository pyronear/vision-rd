# Bbox-tube-temporal precision investigation

Status: design (not yet implemented)
Date: 2026-04-16

## Goal

Close the precision gap between the curated-tube val metrics reported
during training (precision 0.97 for `gru_convnext_finetune`, 0.95 for
`vit_dinov2_finetune`) and the deployment-pipeline metrics observed on
both the leaderboard test split and the newly-added `evaluate_packaged`
val split (precision 0.87 / 0.82 respectively).

Target: **precision ≥ 0.93 at recall ≥ 0.95** on val-packaged, for at
least one packaged variant. That decisively beats
`pyro-detector-baseline` (0.86 / 0.96) and puts the approach within
striking distance of `fsm-tracking-baseline` (0.95 / 0.97).

Time-to-detection is **out of scope** for this investigation; the
winner-tube-end-frame trigger rule that inflates mean TTD to ~515s is
being addressed in a follow-up PR.

## Context: what `evaluate_packaged` told us

Running the packaged `model.zip` through `TemporalModel.predict` on
`data/01_raw/datasets/{train,val}/` (the same splits used to train the
classifier) produced nearly identical precision on train and val and
matching the leaderboard test number:

| variant                    | train (packaged) | val (packaged) | test (leaderboard) |
|----------------------------|------------------|----------------|--------------------|
| `gru_convnext_finetune`    | 0.8624           | 0.8678         | 0.8696             |
| `vit_dinov2_finetune`      | 0.8105           | 0.8226         | 0.8136             |

The triple-match rules out distribution shift as the dominant cause.
The classifier has memorized the training tubes (per-tube train
precision = 0.992), yet running the deployment pipeline on those same
sequences produces 0.86 precision. The gap is pipeline loss, not
generalization.

Offline analysis of `predictions.json` on val-packaged
(`gru_convnext_finetune`):

- 159 FP sequences, 85 yield zero kept tubes; 74 yield at least one.
- Median tubes/seq on non-empty FPs: 2 (mean 2.78, p90 6, max 15).
- Per-tube firing rate on FP sequences (sigmoid ≥ threshold): **23.8%**
  (49 tubes / 206 total).
- Per-tube firing rate on smoke sequences: **83.3%** — one in six
  smoke tubes misses, rescued at sequence level by the
  `argmax ≥ threshold` rule having multiple chances.
- Predicted sequence FPR assuming independence at median_k=2:
  42% — far above the observed 14.5%. Failing tubes within a
  sequence are highly correlated, not independent.

## Root causes (identified from code + data)

1. **Tube selection bias.** Training keeps only the longest tube per
   sequence (`build_tubes.py:114`, `select_longest_tube`). The
   classifier was trained and calibrated on one tube per sequence;
   deployment scores all kept tubes. The classifier has never seen the
   shorter, weaker tubes deployment exposes it to.

2. **Tube-length filter mismatch.** Training filters at
   `build_tubes.min_tube_length = 4`; deployment at
   `tubes.infer_min_tube_length = 2`. Deployment tubes can carry half
   the temporal evidence of training tubes.

3. **Threshold calibrated on the wrong distribution.**
   `calibrate_threshold` picks the smallest probability achieving 95%
   per-tube recall on curated val tubes (one-tube-per-sequence). The
   result (0.5075 for `gru_convnext_finetune`, 0.8265 for
   `vit_dinov2_finetune`) does not generalize to the deployment
   multi-tube tube distribution.

4. **Label source asymmetry.** Training smoke tubes use 5-col GT boxes
   (`source = "gt" if is_wf else "yolo"`, `build_tubes.py:97-98`);
   deployment smoke tubes come from live YOLO. A smoke tube crop from
   a tight GT box ≠ the same sequence's crop through a looser / shifted
   YOLO box. Likely contributes to the 15–17% smoke-tube miss rate.

5. **YOLO-version drift.** Training `.txt` labels were generated by
   historical YOLO weights that may differ from the `best.pt` baked in
   `model.zip`. Confirmed: Pyronear retrains YOLO over time. Even with
   identical confidence thresholds, box geometry drifts between
   training and deployment.

6. **Inference confidence threshold is 0.01.** `params.yaml`
   `package.infer.confidence_threshold: 0.01` lets near-random YOLO
   detections into the tube builder. The confidence used to generate
   training labels is almost certainly higher (typical YOLO default
   ≥ 0.25). Deployment sees a substantially noisier detection stream
   than the classifier trained on.

The 24% per-tube FPR at deployment is the joint effect of (1) + (2)
+ (4) + (5) + (6) producing tubes the classifier never trained on,
evaluated at a threshold (3) that was calibrated on a cleaner
distribution.

## Investigation tracks

Five tracks ordered by cost / reversibility. Execute in the listed
order; each track's results inform the next's necessity.

### Track A — Offline sweeps on existing `predictions.json`

No re-eval, no retraining. Read predictions from
`data/08_reporting/{train,val}/packaged/{variant}/predictions.json`
and explore alternative decision rules over per-tube logits.

Experiments:

- Threshold sweep on `max(tube_logits)` — produce sequence-level PR
  curves and locate the threshold giving ≥ 0.95 sequence recall with
  the highest precision. Compare to the baked threshold.
- Top-k aggregation: require the k highest tube logits to all exceed
  τ, for k ∈ {2, 3}. Does requiring multiple tubes to agree trade
  recall for precision favourably?
- Threshold + min-k combination grid.

Deliverable: a single script producing one markdown report at
`data/08_reporting/aggregation_ablation.md` with tables and curves.
**No code changes to the model.**

Success bar: identifies a drop-in rule with precision ≥ 0.90 at
recall ≥ 0.95 on val-packaged, or conclusively rules out that such a
rule exists.

### Track C — Inference-config alignment (runs before Track B)

Re-run `evaluate_packaged` with two config ablations. Promoted ahead
of Track B because changing the upstream tube population is cheaper
than modifying selection logic and may make tube-selection changes
moot.

Prerequisite: confirm what confidence threshold was used to generate
the training `.txt` labels (inspect `pyro-dataset` preparation scripts
or dataset docs). Without this, the confidence alignment target is a
guess.

Experiments:

- `package.infer.confidence_threshold` raised from `0.01` to the
  training-label value (expected 0.1–0.25). Prunes junk tubes
  upstream.
- `package.infer_min_tube_length` raised from 2 to 4 to match
  `build_tubes.min_tube_length`.

Deliverable: two additional `evaluate_packaged` runs per variant.
Re-run Track A's offline analysis on the new `predictions.json`.

Success bar: same as Track A, measured under the aligned config.

### Track B — Single-tube-at-inference

Only pursued if Tracks A + C leave a ≥ 3pp precision gap to target.

Experiments (require modifying `BboxTubeTemporalModel.predict` to
filter `kept` down to one tube before `score_tubes`):

- Longest-only (direct training replica via `select_longest_tube`).
- Highest-YOLO-confidence-only.

Watch for smoke-recall regression — the longest tube in a smoke
sequence under drifted YOLO may not be the smoke plume.

### Track D — YOLO-version alignment + retrain

Heavy. Only if A + B + C fall short. Regenerate training `.txt`
labels by running the YOLO baked into `model.zip` on the raw
sequences. Rebuild tubes → patches → retrain classifier. Optionally
also switch smoke labels to YOLO-generated boxes to close the
`source = "gt"` asymmetry.

This track is a data-pipeline change and is documented in its own
follow-up spec if we reach it.

### Track E — Multi-instance retraining

Last resort. Train on bags (all tubes per sequence) with
sequence-level supervision. Fundamentally addresses the tube-selection
bias at its source. Documented in its own follow-up spec if reached.

## Success criteria

Investigation concludes successfully when **at least one packaged
variant** reaches **precision ≥ 0.93 at recall ≥ 0.95** on
val-packaged, without regressing train-packaged precision below 0.90
(guards against overfitting to val during rule selection).

A secondary report documents which track(s) were needed and the
ablation breakdown.

## Out of scope

- TTD / trigger-frame-index fix (follow-up PR).
- Leaderboard test re-run until val-packaged hits the precision bar.
- Changes to the `package.target_recall` calibration policy beyond
  Track A's sequence-level re-calibration.
- Architecture changes (new backbone, new head).
- Alternative experiments (MTB, FSM, pyro-detector) — precision gap
  is bbox-tube-temporal-specific.

## Implementation notes

- Track A lives in `bbox-tube-temporal` as
  `scripts/analyze_aggregation_rules.py` (one-off, not wired into
  `dvc.yaml`). Emits a markdown report under
  `data/08_reporting/aggregation_ablation.md`.
- Track C re-runs `evaluate_packaged` with overridden params, which
  rebuilds `model.zip` via the `package` stage. No new stages needed;
  just parameter overrides and `dvc repro`.
- Track B, if pursued, is a small `predict`-time modification behind
  a config flag; the packaged `config.yaml` gains an optional
  `decision.selection_rule` key with a default of `"all"` to preserve
  current behaviour.

## Follow-ups (not part of this investigation)

- TTD fix (first-crossing trigger instead of winner-end-frame).
- Calibration procedure refactor to optimize sequence-level precision
  at a given recall target, not per-tube recall.
- Promote `evaluate_packaged` + Track A's aggregation analysis to a
  standard deliverable for future temporal-model experiments.
